Biography:

I want to be...

An IT technician. I currently work at Winning Strategies ITS in Newark. I have learned extensively about networking, and I hope to one day own my own IT support business. There are endless possibilities with technology, and with everything that is coming out I am eager to see what the possibilities will be years to come from now.

I want to own a separate smaller business that deals just with custom computers/servers. Overkill rigs, crazy watercooling, custom towers. I always wished I had the materials to work with an artist on a computer and make some crazy rigs, and I hope to one day be able to do something like that.

My biggest personal goal is becoming a guitarist. Not with the intention of becoming famous but I think it would be so fun to just play songs that friends and family love, or play in a small band with friends. Most importantly I think learning an instrument teaches a lot more than just music. It lays out the basic understanding that absolutely nothing is going to just come to you. There is no other way around it than to work on something day by day to improve. I recentely picked up skateboarding which I will get to in my biography, but I can take those same lessons from learning music and apply it to anything.

Hobbies:

  • Skateboarding. I just started skating within the last three months, and the most notable thing I can say is that a skateboarding is the most humbling experience. There is no possibility of getting better without falling over and over. But you also find that every time you get up and walk it off, it gets just a little big easier to fall the next time. And when you do finally start to see progress theres nothing more rewarding. The only flat ground trick I have nailed is a pop shuv-it, but I am definitley proud. I skate transition a lot better than I am at tricking, but after the first few months when everything starts coming together it is thereputic in a way. Looking forward to summer to skate.
  • Guitar. I picked up guitar when I was in 4th grade, but never took it seriously. I took lessons for a year or two, but when COVID hit, my teacher sadly passed away. I stopped playing for a while until I reached out to my 4th grade teacher, who at the time knew me for the kid who dressed as Jimmy Page for the school wax museum, and had brought my replica guitar. He swore it looked exactly like one he had in college, and had actually offered me to trade for his stratocaster which was definitely worth a lot more. I had said no, but then senior year of highschool I just decided to email him asking if he wanted to have the guitar given that I wasnt playing anymore. He agreed, and we met up and when I got there he had a Fender Squier Mustang waiting for me. After that I had picked it back up and played more consistently than I ever have. Its a great thing to do in my free time , especially compared to scrolling Instagram reels for 5 hours. I hope to keep playing , and begin truly learning more than just memorizing songs and chords.
  • Building Computers. In 2019, my Dad randomly decided he wanted to teach me to build a computer. He ordered some parts for no more than $600 bucks, but for the time it ran any game I wanted without any struggle so I was thrilled. When he was teaching me and I was watching, I thought I was watching someone build a rocket. After that point I began researching all the things I could do with my PC, whether it was running a Minecraft server for my friends, or learning to upgrade the system itself, I was hooked. I started saving money to make my own home setup, and after about 6 years I can say I am thrilled with how its come out. I have built over 20 systems, many for friends and family including my granda. Fun fact ! He desings and maps out AC units, and one of the newer buildings in I believe the science building here at RV, had its AC vents mapped by him. I have had some luck reselling PC's on facebook, and have made a few thousand. While it is not crazy money, knowing there is a possibilty to support myself doing something I love is a huge motivation.
  • Video Games. I have been playing video games since before I can remember. I used to watch my dad play Bioshock when I was no older than 5, which looking back on it is a little crazy.... But nevertheless he has put me onto some amazing games. When I was 10, I played through Assassins Creed II. That game change my whole perspective on games. There is so many valuable lessons that can be learned through these digital worlds crafted so meticulously. Cyberpunk 2077 is another game that showed me how immersive these worlds can be. Characters that really envoke critical thought throughout the story. And I dont have nearly enough space to even start about Red Dead Redemption 1 and 2. The amount of detail in those games blow my mind. I mean I wont sit here and act like I dont play games because they are fun, but there are also so many things to learn from some of these stories shown in these games.
